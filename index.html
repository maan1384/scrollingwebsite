History of Skateboarding

An interactive long scrolling site on the history of skateboarding.

The first skateboard was conceived by surfers who wanted to surf on concrete.
These are 2x4s with roller skate wheels attached to them.
At the end of the 1970s skateboarding took another hit when the public skateparks that were being built suffered from the outrageously hight insurance rates due to the danger factor of skateboarding.
The Zephyr team consisted of many members, but the most notable of them where Tony Alva, Jay Adams and Stacy Peralta.
Then in 1978 Alan "Ollie" Gelfand invented a maneuver known as the "Ollie" that gave skateboarding another revolutionary jump.
With the invention of the VHS, Stacey Peralta and George Bones Brigade team started recording skateboarding videos.
The team included Steve Caballero, Tony Hawk, Mike McGill, Lance Mountain, Rodney Mullen, Stacy Peralta, and Kevin Staab.
Skateparks pop up everywhere and skateboarding video games lets every kid be a skater. The X-Games continues to become more and more popular with Skateboarding at the helm.
At the end of the 1980s skateboarding took yet another dive in popularity when vert skateboarding became far less popular than street skateboarding. In the early 90s skateboarding starts to rise again as it finds some common ground with the emerging punk music.
Then in 1995 ESPN holds the first ever X-Games. The event was a huge success and brought skateboarding into the mainstream light sparking interest in many more young kids.
